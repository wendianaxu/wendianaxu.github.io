Research

Concrete Data Visualization Designs

Can visualization design choices bring information closer to the viewer?

Traditional forms of data visualizations, such as bar charts, abstract individual data points into aggregate geometric elements. Some visualization practitioners argue that this form of visualization distant the audience from what the data represent in the real world. When used to raise concerns in issues such as human suffering, abstract visualizations can fail to evoke empathy.

In my senior thesis project, I look into how we can design visualizations to proximize data and increase concern. I use the Construal Level Theory in psychology to understand how concrete visualization designs may reduce viewers' psychological distance and induce a concrete mindset.

Psychological Distance of Climate Change and Decision Making

When does bringing climate impacts closer motivate action and when does it backfire?

There is an attitude-behavior gap when it comes to the climate crisis: people are not taking enough action that matches their expressed concerns. One reason could be that the impacts of climate change are not perceived as close and tangible enough to drive behavioral change. We tend to see climate change as influencing distant places in the far future.

Recent research revealed that learning about local climate impacts can make certain groups of individuals less willing to take action. I conducted an independent study to disentangle how individual predispositions, such as value orientations, moderate the effect of psychological distance on climate action.
