I am a senior at Middlebury College studying Psychology and Computer Science, graduating in Februrary 2024.

Many challenges in collective action to address pressing societal and environmental issues today lie in our susceptibility to cognitive biases. We hold on to and strengthen polarized beliefs via confirmation bias and motivated reasoning. We discount consequences that feel distant in space and time, even though they cost more than immediate action.

I am interested in conducting research to understand how we can design information visualization systems to mitigate these biases. I aim to leverage theoretical understandings of visual cognition to design and build tools that alleviate biases in real-world decision making.

Research

Raising Awareness of Natural Disaster Risks With Concrete Data Visualization

Do more concrete forms of visualization bring risks closer?

Traditional forms of information visualizations (e.g., bar charts) abstract individual data points into aggregate geometric elements. Some argue that this could distant the audience from what the data represent in the real world. Construal Level Theory (CLT) in psychology supports this notion by showing that concrete visual representations produce closer perceptions of distance in time and space.

In my senior thesis project, I draw on CLT to investigate how we may utilize concrete data visualization designs to raise awareness of commonly overlooked natural disaster risks by making them feel closer and more certain.

Psychological Distance of Climate Change and Climate Engagement

When does bringing climate impacts closer motivate action and when does it backfire?

People tend to see climate change as influencing distant places in the far future. Does this suggest that communicating local climate impacts can motivate climate support?

Recent research revealed that learning about local climate impacts can make certain groups of individuals less willing to take action. I conducted an independent study to disentangle how one’s value orientations, such as being more egoistic versus altruistic, alter how they react to proximal climate information.
